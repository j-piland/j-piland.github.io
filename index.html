J. Piland

My work lives at the intersection of computer vision, anomaly detection, and AI trustworthiness: I like taking messy, high-stakes real-world problems and turning them into models that are both effective and understandable. Along the way, I’ve bounced from network-based analyses in computational biology (proteins, folding, and structure) to modern deep learning where the “data” can be as slippery as synthetic media.

Lately, I’ve been focused on synthetic face detection and building detectors that don’t just memorize artifacts but actually pay attention to the right signals. In recent work, I explore ideas like entropy-guided training to improve model focus and non-generative, energy-based approaches that offer a different lens on what a model believes (and how confident it is). If you’re into trustworthy AI, detecting generated content, and stress-testing models against clever fakes, we’ll probably get along.

I’m always excited about work that connect theory to practice: safer spoofing detection, more understandable evaluation protocols, and applying network thinking to new domains. If you’re curious, check out my CV and publication list, and feel free to reach out.
